Six facts that changed the modeling design

These are the findings with the largest consequences for feature engineering, validation and model choice.

3,576

Missing product-dates are structural

Most absent rows are staggered product launches, but 29 are isolated gaps inside active histories. Missing row, pre-launch state, observed stockout and true zero demand therefore cannot be collapsed into one value.

5.1% → 45.1%

The app channel migrated rapidly

App share rose from 5.1% in 2021 to 45.1% in 2025. There are 1,501 app-only campaign rows and no web-only campaign rows, indicating deliberate app incentives and channel substitution.

45.50 → 21.81

Recent January demand fell sharply

Mean total demand over January 1–11 fell from 45.50 units in 2024 to 36.56 in 2025 and 21.81 in 2026. Older years could not simply receive equal trust without testing recency controls.

Sun +19.7%

Calendar and retail events dominate

Sunday is strongest, Saturday is 27.6% below average, and November/December reach roughly 1.95×/1.53× normal demand. The seven-day test block conveniently contains one complete Monday-to-Sunday cycle.

25.3%

“Unavailable” does not mean zero

One quarter of unavailable rows still contain positive sales. Availability is absent from test, so it is useful for identifying censored training observations—not as a future predictor or a mechanical zeroing rule.

98.6%

Test covariates were familiar

Nearly all test prices appeared exactly in train, all products and campaign types were known, and no unseen campaign–discount combinations appeared. The primary risk was target nonstationarity, not unseen categories.

What we deliberately did not do

Important non-decisions that prevented convenient but misleading shortcuts.

No random row splitRows close in time share the same retail regime. Every comparison uses chronological rolling origins and a seven-day future block.
No blind zero fillingCalendar gaps, pre-launch periods and unavailable observations remain distinct from genuine observed zero demand.
No ordinal campaign codesSubtype 18 is not “more campaign” than subtype 2. Campaign identifiers use embeddings, native categorical handling or one-hot encoding.
No automatic peak clippingBlack Friday and Christmas extremes are plausible business events. We retain them, add event context and inspect high-volume/error diagnostics instead.

Known limitations

What the supplied data cannot resolve, even with careful modeling.

Availability is only a proxyConditional scoring avoids treating stockouts as demand, but without inventory quantities or lost-sales labels we cannot reconstruct fully unconstrained latent demand.
Rare subtype 5Only a handful of rows exist. Global pooled models reduce variance, but no trustworthy subtype-specific effect can be claimed. It does not occur in the supplied test week.
Limited product metadataThere are only 30 products and no category, brand, traffic, inventory amount or external demand drivers, limiting transfer to unseen products and causal interpretation.
Price anomalies remain observationsLarge one-day price moves were not “corrected” without business confirmation. Relative/effective-price features and availability context reduce dependence on any single raw level.

The resulting forecasting picture

The task became a leakage-safe, global seven-day panel forecast of total demand under strong weekly and annual seasonality, synchronized market shocks, changing channel composition, product lifecycle states and partially censored sales. In this repository that complexity is distilled into one frozen project-specific incumbent and one foundation-model challenger, evaluated under the same contract.
