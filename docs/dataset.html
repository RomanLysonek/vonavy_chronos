Domain evidence that matters to the challenge

Why this is a demanding zero-shot transfer test rather than a large, generic pretraining-like panel.

30 series

Limited domain breadth

Only 30 related products are available. This is enough for a controlled challenge, but little evidence from which a zero-shot model can infer local retail semantics.

5.1% → 45.1%

The app channel migrated rapidly

App share rose from 5.1% in 2021 to 45.1% in 2025. There are 1,501 app-only campaign rows and no web-only campaign rows, indicating deliberate app incentives and channel substitution.

5 cycles

Few repetitions of annual events

Roughly five annual cycles contain the holiday and January regimes. Event effects therefore have limited repetition and substantial local context.

Sun +19.7%

Calendar and retail events dominate

Sunday is strongest, Saturday is 27.6% below average, and November/December reach roughly 1.95×/1.53× normal demand. The seven-day test block conveniently contains one complete Monday-to-Sunday cycle.

25.3%

Stock state is not a simple zero

One quarter of unavailable rows still contain positive sales. Both contenders therefore mask censored history and share the same availability-aware scoring population.

Local context

Retail covariates carry task meaning

Campaign subtype, channel-specific discounts, effective prices and planned calendar events are known to both models, but the incumbent was explicitly designed around their local semantics.

Identical information contract

Neither contender receives a private target row, forecast origin, or future fact.

Observed historyThe same product-date history is available at every rolling origin; gaps and stock-constrained quantities remain masked rather than converted to zero.
Known future rowsBoth models forecast the same 30 × 7 keys with the same calendar, campaign, discount and price information.
Forbidden informationNeither model receives future quantities or realised future availability.
Common score populationMetrics use only rows with one actual and finite predictions from both contenders.

The resulting interpretation

Chronos-2 was not denied information available to Best NN; it was denied task-specific learning by design. The negative result therefore supports a narrow conclusion: on this small, regime-heavy retail panel, the pinned zero-shot challenger did not outperform the already-frozen specialised network.
