Evaluation Contract

What is fixed, what selects the winner, and what is only confirmation.

The central rule

Best NN and Chronos-2 must forecast the same product-date keys from the same information cutoff. WAPE is computed on one common population; a model cannot improve its score by silently dropping difficult rows.

Development origins

Used for model comparison and winner selection

Recent benchmark origins

Held back as the closest pseudo-test confirmation

Scoring layers

Each layer answers a different question; only one is primary.

01

Primary population

Conditional demand

Score only days when the product was available for purchase. Stockouts censor demand and are not treated as true zero demand.

02

Fair comparison

Common rows

A row is scored only when the actual and both contender predictions are finite. Both models therefore receive identical denominators.

03

Primary metric

Global WAPE

Sum of absolute errors divided by sum of actual demand. High-volume product-days receive proportionally appropriate weight.

04

Decision protocol

Development first

The selected winner comes from development OOF. The recent benchmark reports confirmation or disagreement but does not retune the models.

Test-aligned strata

The January test week motivates a weighted development score without discarding broader stress periods.

Leakage boundary

Information allowed at each forecast origin

Observed historyQuantity and availability up to and including the forecast origin.
Known future covariatesTarget-date calendar, campaign, discount and price information supplied in the test frame.
Forbidden future informationTarget quantities, future availability realization, and any lag or rolling value that would require post-origin demand.
Chronos-specific guardStockouts and synthetic gaps are masked as missing history; future_df never receives quantities or realized availability.
